# Contributor Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We, as maintainers, contributors, and community members of Mana-Agent, are
committed to creating an open, welcoming, respectful, and safe environment for
everyone who participates in the project.

We expect all participants to act with integrity, empathy, and professionalism,
regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex
characteristics, gender identity or expression, level of experience, education,
socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, sexual
identity, or sexual orientation.

Our goal is to build a healthy technical community where people can collaborate,
experiment, learn, and contribute without harassment or intimidation.

## Expected Behavior

Behavior that helps strengthen the Mana-Agent community includes:

* Treating other people with kindness, patience, and respect.
* Respecting different technical opinions, experiences, and backgrounds.
* Giving constructive, actionable, and professional feedback.
* Accepting constructive feedback without hostility or retaliation.
* Taking responsibility for mistakes and working to correct their impact.
* Respecting the purpose, architecture, and long-term health of the project.
* Communicating technical disagreements based on evidence and engineering
  considerations rather than personal attacks.
* Protecting user privacy, credentials, secrets, and sensitive project data.
* Reporting security concerns responsibly.
* Clearly identifying significant risks, limitations, or uncertainty when
  contributing AI-generated code, analysis, or documentation.
* Helping create an environment where new contributors can participate and
  learn.

## Unacceptable Behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable within the Mana-Agent community:

* Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained hostile behavior.
* Trolling, insulting, humiliating, or derogatory comments.
* Personal, discriminatory, or targeted political attacks.
* Sexualized language, imagery, attention, or unwanted advances.
* Deliberately disrupting discussions, issues, pull requests, or community
  spaces.
* Repeatedly contacting another person after being asked to stop.
* Publishing or exposing another person's private information without explicit
  permission.
* Exposing credentials, API keys, access tokens, private repository data, or
  other secrets.
* Knowingly submitting malicious code, backdoors, credential-stealing logic,
  spyware, or intentionally destructive behavior.
* Using project tools, agents, or automation to harass, impersonate, deceive, or
  intentionally harm another person.
* Falsifying test results, security findings, benchmarks, authorship, or
  technical evidence.
* Deliberately presenting AI-generated output as verified when the contributor
  knows it has not been reviewed or validated.
* Retaliating against someone who reports a concern in good faith.
* Other conduct that a reasonable person would consider inappropriate in a
  professional open-source community.

## Technical and AI Agent Conduct

Mana-Agent is an AI-assisted software engineering project. Contributors may use
AI models, coding agents, automation, and external development tools.

The use of these systems does not remove a contributor's responsibility for
their contribution.

Contributors are expected to:

* Review generated code before submitting it.
* Avoid intentionally introducing insecure or destructive agent behavior.
* Avoid placing real secrets or private user information in tests, examples,
  prompts, logs, or issue reports.
* Clearly document significant security implications of new tools or
  permissions.
* Respect tool and repository access boundaries.
* Avoid misleading claims about model capabilities, verification, or test
  coverage.
* Cooperate with maintainers when generated changes create regressions or
  security risks.

A contributor remains responsible for content submitted through their account,
including content produced with the assistance of an AI model or autonomous
agent.

## Community Spaces

This Code of Conduct applies to all official Mana-Agent community spaces,
including:

* GitHub repositories.
* Issues and discussions.
* Pull requests and code reviews.
* Project documentation.
* Official chat or communication channels.
* Project-managed websites and services.
* Community events and technical meetings.

It also applies when an individual is officially representing Mana-Agent or
Mana Development in a public space.

Examples include using an official email address, posting through an official
project account, speaking as an appointed project representative, or
participating in an event on behalf of the project.

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers and community leaders are responsible for interpreting and
enforcing this Code of Conduct fairly and consistently.

Maintainers may remove, edit, hide, or reject comments, commits, code, issues,
pull requests, documentation, or other contributions that violate these
standards.

When appropriate, maintainers will explain moderation decisions.

Maintainers must handle reports in good faith and take reasonable steps to
protect the privacy and security of reporters and affected individuals.

A maintainer who has a direct conflict of interest involving a report should,
when reasonably possible, allow another maintainer to review the matter.

## Reporting an Incident

Abusive, harassing, threatening, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be
reported to the Mana-Agent community maintainers at:

**[root@manadev.net](mailto:root@manadev.net)**

Security vulnerabilities should also be reported privately when public
disclosure could expose users, credentials, repositories, systems, or services
to risk.

Reports should include relevant details when available, such as:

* A description of the incident.
* Where the incident occurred.
* Relevant dates or approximate times.
* Links, screenshots, logs, or other supporting information.
* Any immediate safety or security concern.

Do not include unnecessary private information or credentials in a report.

Reports will be reviewed as promptly and fairly as reasonably possible.

The privacy and security of reporters will be respected. Information concerning
an incident will only be shared when reasonably necessary to investigate,
protect affected people, or enforce this Code of Conduct.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will consider the context, severity, frequency, intent, and impact of
a violation when deciding on an appropriate response.

The following guidelines describe common enforcement levels.

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Minor inappropriate, disruptive, or unprofessional
behavior.

**Possible Response:** A private written correction explaining the problematic
behavior and the expected change.

The contributor may be asked to edit or remove content, correct a contribution,
or provide an apology when appropriate.

### 2. Formal Warning

**Community Impact:** A clear violation of community standards or repeated
behavior after a correction.

**Possible Response:** A formal warning describing the violation and the
consequences of continued behavior.

Restrictions may be placed on interactions with affected individuals or in
specific community spaces for a defined period.

Failure to respect these restrictions may result in a temporary or permanent
ban.

### 3. Temporary Restriction or Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation, sustained inappropriate behavior, or
continued violations following a warning.

**Possible Response:** Temporary removal of participation privileges.

During this period, the individual may not participate in specified project
spaces or contact affected individuals regarding the incident.

Attempts to bypass the restriction through alternate accounts or third parties
may result in a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Severe misconduct or a pattern of behavior that creates
significant risk or harm to individuals or the community.

Examples may include sustained harassment, credible threats, malicious
technical activity, repeated retaliation, or persistent violations after prior
enforcement actions.

**Possible Response:** Permanent removal from public participation in the
Mana-Agent community.

Where technically appropriate, repository, organization, communication, or
service access may also be revoked.

## Enforcement Decisions

Enforcement decisions should be based on the available evidence and the
reasonable impact of the reported conduct.

Good-faith disagreement, technical criticism, rejected contributions, or
different architectural opinions are not automatically Code of Conduct
violations.

Knowingly false or malicious reports may themselves be considered a violation
of this Code of Conduct.

Nothing in this Code of Conduct prevents maintainers from taking immediate
action when they reasonably believe a security threat, credible safety risk, or
ongoing serious harm requires urgent intervention.
